Research Topic

Substance Use Disorder Treatment and Recovery Services in Puerto Rico: Cultural Influences on Addiction, Recovery, and Community Support

This creative project transforms research into a mock client and community brochure for a proposed recovery agency. Instead of presenting the information as a traditional essay, the research is woven into a public-facing treatment and recovery concept that is relevant to chemical dependency counseling, human services, public health, and culturally responsive care.

Client & Community Brochure

Welcome to Proyecto Esperanza Recovery Network™

Culture is the Treatment. Community is the Medicine.

Bienvenidos. Proyecto Esperanza Recovery Network™ is a proposed culturally responsive behavioral health organization designed to serve Puerto Rican individuals and families throughout the United States. The agency concept combines evidence-based addiction treatment with family, culture, language, spirituality, community, and hope.

Recovery is about more than overcoming substance use. It is about reconnecting with identity, restoring family relationships, addressing trauma and mental health, reducing barriers to care, and building a meaningful life within the community.

For Clients

A welcoming place to begin recovery, receive support, rebuild family connections, and create a personalized Cultural Recovery Plan™.

For Families

A recovery partner that includes parents, grandparents, children, chosen family, and loved ones in the healing process.

For Communities

A culturally grounded recovery network designed to strengthen health, belonging, prevention, and long-term stability.

Ten Research-Based Brochure Sections

Each section below represents one research-informed part of the brochure and directly connects the academic research to the agency concept.

1Our Mission

Substance use disorders affect individuals, families, and entire communities. Puerto Rican communities often face additional challenges related to healthcare access, economic hardship, language, migration, and cultural barriers. Proyecto Esperanza was designed to address these challenges through culturally responsive addiction treatment that recognizes recovery as both a medical and community process. Our mission is to restore health while honoring culture, family, and identity. (Caetano et al.; Hansen)

2Recovery Starts with Family

Strong family relationships are among the greatest protective factors against addiction and relapse. Proyecto Esperanza actively involves parents, grandparents, siblings, children, and chosen family members throughout treatment because recovery is strongest when loved ones heal together. Family counseling, education, and recovery celebrations are integrated into every level of care. By treating family as part of the solution, the agency supports both individual recovery and intergenerational healing. (Caetano et al. 2017; Caetano et al. 2019)

3Removing Barriers to Treatment

Many people never begin treatment because of transportation difficulties, unstable housing, financial hardship, stigma, or confusion about navigating healthcare systems. Proyecto Esperanza responds with bilingual case management, transportation assistance, peer navigators, housing support, and open-access admissions. These services are designed to reduce the practical barriers that often prevent clients from entering or remaining in care. (Abadie et al. 2021; Leff et al.)

4Healing Mind and Body Together

Substance use disorders frequently occur alongside depression, anxiety, trauma, suicidal ideation, and other mental health conditions. Proyecto Esperanza uses integrated care teams that coordinate addiction counseling, psychiatric services, medical care, and trauma-informed treatment. This approach recognizes that recovery is stronger when emotional health, physical health, and substance use treatment are addressed together. (Collazo et al.; Pérez-Pedrogo et al.)

5Culture as a Source of Healing

Puerto Rican culture offers powerful sources of strength through music, food, storytelling, language, spirituality, family loyalty, and community connection. Proyecto Esperanza incorporates cultural expression into treatment through family meals, music, art, community celebrations, and culturally meaningful discussion groups. These elements are not used as decoration; they become part of how clients rebuild identity and belonging. (Hansen 2004; Hansen 2012)

6Disaster Resilience and Continuity of Care

Natural disasters such as Hurricane Maria showed how quickly treatment services can be interrupted and how disruption can increase relapse risk. Proyecto Esperanza would build emergency continuity into its model through telehealth, mobile outreach, community partnerships, peer check-ins, and flexible service delivery. Recovery support must remain available during crisis, displacement, and community instability. (Contreras et al.)

7Harm Reduction Saves Lives

Recovery begins wherever a person is today. Harm reduction services such as naloxone education, fentanyl test strips, overdose prevention, and immediate treatment connection help keep people alive while opening the door to long-term recovery. Proyecto Esperanza treats harm reduction as a bridge to healing rather than as a separate or less valuable service. (Reyes Orlando et al.; Abadie and Fisher)

8The Cultural Recovery Plan™

Each client develops a Cultural Recovery Plan™ alongside a traditional clinical treatment plan. This plan identifies family traditions, language preferences, community connections, spiritual beliefs, personal values, and cultural strengths that may support recovery. By recognizing culture as a source of resilience, treatment becomes more personal, meaningful, and sustainable. (Caetano et al.; Hansen)

9Recovery Beyond Discharge

Long-term recovery requires continued connection after formal treatment ends. Proyecto Esperanza includes peer recovery coaching, alumni groups, housing partnerships, educational referrals, employment support, volunteer opportunities, and family follow-up. These services help clients remain connected to recovery while building stability, independence, and purpose in the community. (Leff et al.; Pérez-Pedrogo et al.)

10A New Vision for Recovery

Proyecto Esperanza represents a proposed model for culturally responsive behavioral healthcare that combines evidence-based treatment with family engagement, integrated mental health services, harm reduction, community partnerships, and cultural identity. The goal is not simply to help people stop using substances, but to help individuals rediscover hope, purpose, belonging, and a healthier future. (Abadie et al.; Caetano et al.; Hansen)

Proposed Levels of Care

Prevention & Education

School, family, church, and neighborhood outreach designed to reduce risk and strengthen protective factors.

Open Access Assessment

Walk-in screening, placement, bilingual support, transportation planning, and insurance navigation.

Medical Detoxification

Withdrawal management, medical monitoring, psychiatric evaluation, and stabilization.

Residential Stabilization

Twenty-four-hour recovery support, counseling, peer mentoring, and family engagement.

Residential Rehabilitation

Structured clinical care, cultural programming, trauma treatment, vocational support, and recovery coaching.

PHP / IOP / Outpatient

Flexible treatment options that support work, school, parenting, and community reintegration.

Medication-Assisted Treatment

Evidence-based medication support combined with counseling, peer support, and ongoing monitoring.

Recovery Housing & Mobile Outreach

Safe housing pathways, community outreach, harm reduction, and linkage to continuing care.

The Cultural Recovery Plan™

Every client receives a clinical treatment plan and a Cultural Recovery Plan™.

The Cultural Recovery Plan™ is the signature feature of Proyecto Esperanza. It helps clients identify cultural, family, spiritual, and community strengths that support long-term recovery.
